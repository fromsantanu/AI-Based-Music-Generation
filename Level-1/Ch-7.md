## 🎵 Chapter 1.7 — Exercises: Generate, Compare, Analyze

---

### 🧠 1. Purpose of This Chapter

Till now, you have learned:

* What AI music is
* How Suno works
* How to write prompts
* How to judge quality

👉 Now it’s time to **practice like a creator**

This chapter is fully **hands-on**.

---

### 🎤 2. Real-Life Analogy

Think of learning music 🎼

* You don’t become good by just reading
* You improve by **playing, listening, and correcting**

👉 Same here:

* Generate → Listen → Compare → Improve

---

## 🔁 3. The Core Practice Loop

This is your main workflow:

👉 **Step 1: Generate a song**
👉 **Step 2: Generate another version**
👉 **Step 3: Compare both**
👉 **Step 4: Analyze differences**
👉 **Step 5: Improve prompt**

💡 This loop is the **heart of AI music creation**

---

## 🧪 4. Exercise 1 — Same Idea, Two Versions

---

### 🎯 Task

Use the same idea but create two prompts:

---

#### Version A (Simple):

> “Create a happy Hindi song about friendship”

---

#### Version B (Detailed):

> “Create a cheerful Hindi friendship song with acoustic guitar, light drums, and warm male vocals celebrating school memories”

---

### 🎧 What to Do

* Generate both
* Listen carefully

---

### 🔍 Analyze

Ask:

* Which one feels more real?
* Which one has better emotion?
* Which one is more detailed?

👉 You will clearly see:
**Better prompt → Better output**

---

## 🧪 5. Exercise 2 — Change One Element Only

---

### 🎯 Base Prompt:

> “Create a sad Hindi song with piano and male vocals about lost love”

---

### 🔄 Now Create Variations:

---

#### Variation 1 (Change Mood):

> “Create a melancholic Hindi song with piano and male vocals about lost love”

---

#### Variation 2 (Change Instruments):

> “Create a sad Hindi song with guitar and violin and male vocals about lost love”

---

#### Variation 3 (Change Voice):

> “Create a sad Hindi song with piano and soft female vocals about lost love”

---

### 🔍 Analyze

👉 Compare all versions:

* How does mood change the feeling?
* How do instruments change the texture?
* How does voice change emotion?

---

## 🧪 6. Exercise 3 — Quality Comparison

---

### 🎯 Task

Generate **2–3 songs** using the same prompt:

> “Create a romantic Hindi song with piano and soft vocals about memories”

---

### 🎧 Now Evaluate Each Version

Use this checklist:

* Mood correct? ✔️/❌
* Voice clear? ✔️/❌
* Lyrics meaningful? ✔️/❌
* Music smooth? ✔️/❌

---

👉 Choose:
**Best version + Why**

---

## 🧪 7. Exercise 4 — Fix a Bad Output

---

### 🎯 Step 1: Use a weak prompt

> “Make a song”

---

### 🎯 Step 2: Observe problems

You may notice:

* No clear mood
* Random style
* Weak lyrics

---

### 🎯 Step 3: Improve it

Rewrite as:

> “Create a calm Bengali song with flute and soft female vocals about rain and memories”

---

👉 Generate again and compare

---

## 🧪 8. Exercise 5 — Create Your Own Style

---

### 🎯 Task

Create 3 different songs based on your preference:

---

#### Song 1:

Your favorite mood

---

#### Song 2:

Your favorite style

---

#### Song 3:

Your favorite instruments

---

👉 Try to notice patterns:

* What do you like most?
* What kind of songs you generate naturally?

💡 This is how your **creative identity** starts forming

---

## 🔍 9. How to Analyze Like a Pro (Simple Method)

Whenever you listen, think in 3 layers:

---

### Layer 1: Emotion

👉 What do I feel?

---

### Layer 2: Sound

👉 What do I hear? (instruments, voice)

---

### Layer 3: Meaning

👉 What is the song saying?

---

👉 If all three are strong → High quality output

---

## 📌 10. Key Takeaways

* Practice is the most important step
* Always compare multiple outputs
* Change one thing at a time
* Analyze using:

  * Emotion
  * Sound
  * Meaning
* Your style develops through repetition
